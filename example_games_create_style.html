Scaling Factor (Zoom):
This setting changes the overall scale of the app.   It's like a zoom level.   The lower the value, the smaller or more "zoomed out" your gameroom will look.   0.7 is the default value (equivalent to 70% zoom level -- like being slightly zoomed out).   1.0 would be no zoom at all.

Global Game Settings:
Any CSS properties specified here will override properties set within the games themselves. This is useful for giving a more uniform look to your gameroom. For example, if you want all of your games to have the same narrow score boxes, you can enter the following into the "CSS for All Score Boxes" field: 
height: 80px; min-height: 80px; max-height: 80px;
It's also useful if you have a large gameroom and wish to set a single font family and size for all of your games at once.

Content Cycler Details and Caveats

The Content Cycler works by creating an iFrame within the page, and loading content into that iFrame. This creates several fundamental limitations:

1. iScored can only cycle through content that is hosted on secure pages (i.e. any URL that begins with "https://"). URLs that begin with "http://" will not load, because you cannot mix secure and unsecure content.

2. iScored can auto-play videos, but only if they have no audio or the audio is muted. Browsers do not allow websites to automatically play audio without user interaction.

Stern Leaderboard Synchronization

Wait, What is This?
If you have a Stern Insider Connected Pro or All-Access account, you can create leaderboards that are accessible through a kiosk URL. If you provide that URL to iScored, then the scores from that leaderboard can also be displayed in iScored, alongside any other games in your iScored gameroom.

What Does the Synchronize Button do, Exactly?
When you provide a Stern Leaderboard Kiosk URL and click "Sync Now", iScored checks what games are in that leaderboard, and creates corresponding games in iScored for each one. These games will be in addition to any games you already have in your iScored gameroom (so if you already have, for example, a King Kong in iScored, it won't replace it, it will create another one). These special "Synced" games can be edited like normal iScored games - you can upload custom graphics or set custom fonts and colors. However, you cannot change their name, nor can you enter scores for them via iScored. Scores for synced games can come only from Stern Insider Connected.

It's also worth noting that no Stern player names or scores are ever imported or stored in iScored's database.

OK, Then How Are the Stern Scores Shown in iScored?
When your iScored gameroom display loads, it pulls the scores and names fresh from Stern, and updates them every 10 seconds or so.

What Does "Remove Sync" Do?
The "Remove Sync" button tells iScored to stop trying to retrieve data from a Stern Leaderboard. Note that it also permanently removes those games from your gameroom. If you wish to sync a new leaderboard URL, but don't want to lose any changes you made to the graphics or formating of your games, then you can simply press the "Sync Now" button without hitting "Remove Sync" first.

What Do These Mean?

Infinite Scroll (Default)
Infinitely scrolls the game columns from left to right, through all the pages in your gameroom. When it reaches the last game, the first game appears, then the second, and so on.

Swing
This method is the same as Infinite Scroll, except that it scrolls the width of one game at a time, and then pauses. The length of the pause is determined by the Animation Speed setting -- the lower the speed, the longer the pause. This method may help obscure stuttering or jagged animation on devices with lower capabilities.

Basic
This method simply scrolls left to right until it reaches the end, then reverses direction. It uses the least processing by far, so if your issue is that the scroll is stuttering or struggling, this might be your best option. However, games at the far left and far right will be seen less often than other games in the game room, since it reverses direciton when it reaches them. Also note that if you have more than 200 games in your gameroom, this option will only scroll through the currently-selected page of games.
